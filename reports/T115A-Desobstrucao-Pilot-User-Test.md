# T115A — Desobstrução Pilot User Test (8–12 Players)

**Date:** March 30, 2026  
**Phase:** Early Signal Validation  
**Scope:** 8–12 real player sessions  
**Goal:** Gate decision for full-scale T115 testing

---

## Executive Summary

T114 completed the audio + impact polish pass. Before scaling to the full 20–50 player T115 wave, this pilot test validates whether the toy factor **survives first contact with real players**.

**Pilot mission:** Confirm no obvious UX/friction blockers exist, measure 4 early signals, and decide whether Desobstrução is healthy enough to scale.

**Expected outcome:** SCALE TO FULL T115 TESTING (if toy factor confirmed + no major friction)

---

## Pilot Testing Protocol

### Phase 1: Recruitment (2–3 hours)

#### Target Sample: 8–12 Players
Mix player types to catch friction early:

**Mobile-First Cohort (3–4 players)**
- Primary device: Smartphone (iOS or Android)
- Platform preference: Mobile apps, no desktop gaming
- Why: Haptics + touch responsiveness must feel good here
- Recruitment: Ask friends/coworkers who play primarily on phone

**Desktop-First Cohort (3–4 players)**
- Primary device: Laptop/PC
- Platform preference: Browser games, some desktop gaming
- Why: Audio + trajectory clarity need to shine on large screen
- Recruitment: Ask coworkers or gaming communities

**Casual / Low-Context Cohort (2–3 players)**
- Platform: Mixed (phone or desktop, doesn't matter)
- Gaming habit: Plays 2–5 hours/week (not hardcore gamers)
- Context familiarity: Little/no knowledge of Bairro Resiste, infrastructure themes
- Why: Catch if toy factor is strong enough to work without lore context
- Recruitment: Ask family members, non-gamer friends

#### Recruitment Checklist
- [ ] Confirm device + browser (iOS Safari, Android Chrome, Desktop Chrome)
- [ ] Confirm basic internet connectivity (testing over 4G is acceptable)
- [ ] Get audio permission (they'll use speakers/headphones)
- [ ] Optional: Ask if they've played Angry Birds / Cut the Rope before

### Phase 2: Session Setup (5 min per player)

#### Before Play
1. Open `/arcade/desobstrucao` on their device
2. **Quick orientation** (don't over-explain):
   - "This is a physics puzzle game. Your goal is to destroy the barrier with a projectile."
   - "Fire it at different angles and power levels to clear the pieces."
   - "No right answer—just try to destroy everything."
3. **Audio/haptics check:**
   - "Turn up volume and make sure you hear the sounds when you fire."
   - "If on mobile, you should feel a light tap when you hit the barrier."
4. **Play window:** Let them play for 2–5 minutes naturally (don't interrupt)

#### During Play
- **Observe silently** (don't comment or guide)
- **Log:**
  - First attempt aiming: Confused? Immediate? Second-guessing?
  - Hit/miss ratio: Ball-parking competence
  - Audio reaction: Do they react to sounds? Smile? Grimace?
  - Retry decisiveness: Do they want to play again immediately?
  - Any verbalized friction: "This is confusing..." / "I want to try again!"

#### After Play (2 min survey)
Give them a quick post-play questionnaire (see below).

---

### Phase 3: Four Early Signal Metrics

#### Metric 1: Retry Rate
**Question:** "Would you play another round right now?"  
**Scale:** Yes / Maybe / No  
**Target:** >70% yes/maybe combined  
**Why:** Immediate replay desire = authentic toy factor

#### Metric 2: Fun Factor
**Question:** "On a scale of 1–10, how fun was that?"  
**Scale:** 1–10  
**Target:** ≥7 average  
**Why:** Baseline enjoyment confirmation

#### Metric 3: Aiming Confidence
**Question:** "Did you feel confident when aiming? (1–5 scale)"  
**Scale:** 1 (confusing) → 5 (intuitive)  
**Target:** ≥4 average  
**Why:** Core interaction must feel responsive

#### Metric 4: Would Share
**Question:** "Would you share this with friends?"  
**Scale:** Yes / Maybe / No  
**Target:** >50% yes/maybe combined  
**Why:** Viral potential signal

#### Bonus Observations
- **Audio satisfaction:** Did they notice/like the sounds? (yes/no/neutral)
- **Haptic feedback:** Did they feel vibrations? (yes/no/N/A)
- **Device friction:** Any interaction problems on their device?
- **Biggest blocker:** If nothing felt great, what was the biggest issue?

---

### Phase 4: Immediate Blocker Flags

Watch for these red flags during play. **If 3+ players flag the same issue, pivot to Polish Pass 2.**

| Blocker | Signal | Action If Flagged |
|---------|--------|------------------|
| **Confusing aiming** | Player struggles to aim after 3rd attempt | Ask: "Does trajectory feel clear?" |
| **Weak impact feeling** | No visible reaction to hits; sounds muted or unclear | Check: Audio volume? Impact flash visible? |
| **Audio annoyance** | Player turns off sound or says "too loud/harsh" | Document frequency + volume |
| **Mobile discomfort** | Thumb cramping, controls feel sluggish, or "off" | Check: Touch responsiveness? Reticle size OK? |
| **Fast boredom** | Plays 1–2 attempts then "done, not interested" | Ask: "What would make this more fun?" |
| **Haptic confusion** | On mobile but doesn't notice or want haptics off | Clarify: Vibration purpose, test pattern |

## Gate Decision Framework

After pilot data collection, evaluate against 3 gates:

### Gate 1: Toy Factor Survival
**Criteria:**
- Retry rate ≥70% (YES/MAYBE combined)? ✓/✗
- Fun factor ≥7.0 average? ✓/✗
- Would share ≥50%? ✓/✗

**If:** 2–3 gates pass → **Proceed**  
**If:** 0–1 gates pass → **Iterate or Pause**

### Gate 2: No Critical Friction
**Criteria:**
- <3 players flag same blocker? ✓/✗
- Aiming confidence ≥4.0? ✓/✗
- No "unusable on [device]" complaints? ✓/✗

**If:** All 3 pass → **Proceed**  
**If:** Any fail → **One Polish Pass Required**

### Gate 3: Device Readiness
**Criteria:**
- iOS working? ✓/✗
- Android working? ✓/✗
- Desktop working? ✓/✗
- Audio/haptics functional? ✓/✗

**If:** 3–4 criteria pass → **Proceed**  
**If:** <3 pass → **Fix blockers before scaling**

## Decision Outcomes

### Outcome A: SCALE TO FULL T115 TESTING
**When:** All 3 gates pass  
**Action:** Launch full 20–50 player testing wave (use T115 protocol)  
**Timeline:** Week 1–2  
**Expected result:** FLAGSHIP CANDIDATE classification

### Outcome B: DO ONE MORE MICRO POLISH PASS (T114 Iteration)
**When:** Gate 1 or Gate 2 partial fail (specific friction identified)  
**Action:** Address specific pilot friction (audio tuning, aiming clarity, etc.)  
**Timeline:** 2–3 days quick fixes  
**Then:** Re-run pilot with same 8–12 players (confirm fix)

### Outcome C: PAUSE THE PHYSICS LANE
**When:** Multiple gates fail + core toy factor ≤6.0  
**Action:** Post-mortem analysis, consider redesign or deprioritize  
**Timeline:** 1 week analysis  
**Expected:** Decision on pivot/retry

## Pilot Philosophy

This pilot is **small, fast, and real**.

- **Small:** 8–12 players (1-2 days of sessions)
- **Fast:** Collect data quickly, decide quickly (don't over-analyze)
- **Real:** Actual players with no coaching (watch for confusion)

**Not a rigorous study.** This is an **early signal gate**—catch if the toy factor is fundamentally broken before investing in a larger test.

**Red line:** If you see >2 players abandon mid-play or say "this isn't fun," pause and iterate.

**Green line:** If you see >70% retry rate and >50% would share, you're ready to scale.
